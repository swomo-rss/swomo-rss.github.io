Structured World Models for Robotic Manipulation

RSS 2025 | Los Angeles, USA

Overview

Modeling and predicting the dynamics of the world is fundamental to robotic manipulation, enabling applications such as sim-to-real learning, model-predictive control, manipulation planning, and model-based design and verification. Traditionally, physics-based models have required extensive manual effort to construct scene-specific representations. Recent breakthroughs in generative modeling—particularly in NLP and computer vision—offer an opportunity to develop structured world models that capture dynamics more flexibly and generalize across diverse scenarios.

This workshop will focus on leveraging structured representations to enhance the accuracy, generalization, and efficiency of world models for robotic manipulation. Key topics include designing effective state and action representations, determining the sources and structure of supervision, and incorporating inductive biases for improved generalization without sacrificing scalability. We will discuss the role of (generative) simulation, the potential of video-based foundation models, and the opportunities and the trade-off of different approaches on their downstream applications in planning, control, and policy learning and evaluation.

The timeliness of this discussion is underscored by rapid advancements in generative and foundation models. To foster an interdisciplinary discussion, the workshop will bring together researchers from robotics, computer vision, machine learning, and cognitive science. The workshop is intended for researchers and practitioners in robotic manipulation, world modeling, reinforcement learning, and sim-to-real learning. We will incorporate interactive elements, including poster sessions, open discussion panels, and live polls to facilitate debate on key challenges and to encourage dialogue between junior and senior researchers. Attendees will have the opportunity to contribute actively to discussions, shaping the future of structured world modeling in robotics.
